## Business Overview
TCS is India's largest IT services company by revenue and market
capitalization, part of the Tata Group. It provides consulting, technology,
and business process services across banking, retail, manufacturing,
telecom, and life sciences, operating in 50+ countries with a workforce of
over 600,000 employees.

## Competitive Moat
- **Switching costs**: Large enterprise clients run mission-critical systems
  on TCS platforms; migrating vendors is costly and risky, producing sticky,
  multi-year contracts.
- **Scale and delivery model**: The Global Delivery Model — a large offshore
  talent base in India combined with onsite client teams — lets TCS
  underprice smaller competitors while maintaining margins.
- **Brand and trust**: Decades-long relationships with Fortune 500 clients
  and a reputation for reliability act as a barrier to entry for new
  competitors chasing large contracts.
- **Diversification**: Revenue is spread across many industries and
  geographies, reducing single-client or single-sector risk.

## Capital Allocation
TCS has historically maintained a near debt-free balance sheet, returns
a high proportion of free cash flow to shareholders via dividends and share
buybacks, and has consistently generated return on equity (ROE) in the
35-45% range over the past decade.

## Management Quality
Leadership has a long tenure, promotes primarily from within, and has kept
messaging around capital discipline and client-centricity consistent across
multiple CEO transitions — a signal value investors often look for.

## Risks
- Revenue concentration in BFSI (banking, financial services, insurance)
  and North America.
- Wage inflation and attrition in the Indian IT sector.
- Exposure to global tech spending cycles and potential disruption from
  generative AI reducing traditional staffing-based IT services demand.
