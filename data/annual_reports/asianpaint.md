## Business Overview
Asian Paints is India's largest paint company, with decorative paints as
its core business, plus a smaller industrial coatings and home
decor/services segment (waterproofing, kitchens, bath fittings).

## Competitive Moat
- **Distribution network**: A vast, decades-built dealer and retail
  network (tens of thousands of touchpoints) that is very difficult and
  slow for new entrants to replicate.
- **Brand loyalty**: Strong consumer trust built over 70+ years, reinforced
  by heavy advertising and a "one-stop-shop" positioning (paint, painting
  services, home decor).
- **Supply chain and colour technology**: In-house manufacturing scale and
  proprietary tinting/colour-matching systems used across the dealer
  network raise switching costs for retailers.
- **Pricing power**: Historically able to pass on raw material (crude
  derivative) cost inflation to consumers with limited volume loss.

## Capital Allocation
Historically very high return on capital employed (ROCE), low debt, and a
long track record of steady dividend growth — a textbook "compounder"
profile that value investors have favored for years.

## Management Quality
Professional management (not purely promoter-run day-to-day), long-tenured
leadership in key functions, and a conservative approach to diversification.

## Risks
- Intensifying competition, notably from a large new entrant (Grasim
  Industries' Birla Opus) investing aggressively in the paints category,
  pressuring market share and margins.
- Raw material cost volatility (crude oil derivatives).
- Premium valuation historically leaves little margin of safety if growth
  or margins disappoint.
