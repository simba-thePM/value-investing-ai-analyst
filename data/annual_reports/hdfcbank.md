## Business Overview
HDFC Bank is India's largest private-sector bank by assets, offering retail
banking, wholesale banking, and treasury operations. Following its 2023
merger with parent HDFC Ltd (a housing finance company), it also carries a
large mortgage book.

## Competitive Moat
- **Low-cost deposit franchise (CASA)**: A large base of current and
  savings account deposits gives HDFC Bank a structurally lower cost of
  funds than most competitors.
- **Distribution network**: One of the largest branch and ATM networks
  among private banks, reinforced by a strong digital banking platform.
- **Underwriting discipline**: Historically among the lowest non-performing
  asset (NPA) ratios in Indian banking, reflecting conservative credit
  underwriting.
- **Cross-sell**: A broad product suite (loans, credit cards, insurance
  distribution, wealth management) increases revenue per customer over
  time.

## Capital Allocation
The bank has consistently compounded book value per share at a high rate
over the long term, funds growth primarily through retained earnings, and
pays a modest but growing dividend.

## Management Quality
Known for conservative, process-driven management and a strong risk
culture, though the 2023 merger with HDFC Ltd introduced a period of
integration risk (credit costs, and matching low-cost deposits to a larger
mortgage book) that investors have been watching closely.

## Risks
- Integration risk from the HDFC Ltd merger, including funding a larger
  mortgage/wholesale-liability mix with retail deposits.
- Sensitivity to interest rate cycles and RBI regulatory policy.
- Rising competition from digital-native NBFCs and fintechs in retail
  lending.
