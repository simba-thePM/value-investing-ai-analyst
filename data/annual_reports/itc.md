## Business Overview
ITC is a diversified conglomerate with five segments: cigarettes (FMCG -
cigarettes), other FMCG (packaged foods, personal care, stationery, under
brands like Aashirvaad, Sunfeast, Bingo, Yippee), hotels (demerged in 2025
into ITC Hotels), paperboard/packaging, and agri-business.

## Competitive Moat
- **Cigarettes cash engine**: The cigarette business carries very high
  margins and generates the bulk of profit, funding investment in the
  lower-margin FMCG-others businesses.
- **Distribution**: One of India's largest FMCG distribution networks,
  reaching both urban and deep-rural markets, which newer FMCG entrants
  struggle to match.
- **Brand portfolio**: Multiple market-leading or top-3 brands across food
  categories built over decades.
- **Backward integration**: Owns significant agri-sourcing and paperboard
  manufacturing capacity, giving cost and supply-chain advantages.

## Capital Allocation
Known historically for a high dividend payout ratio (a key reason
value/income investors hold it), though capital allocation into the
FMCG-others segment took many years to become profitable and was a
frequent point of investor criticism.

## Management Quality
Professionally managed, though ownership is diffuse (no dominant promoter),
which has both pros (governance, professional management) and cons
(historically slower capital allocation decisions cited by activist
investors).

## Risks
- Regulatory and taxation risk: cigarettes are subject to high and
  unpredictable excise/GST taxation changes in India, directly impacting
  the core profit engine.
- ESG/exclusion risk: some institutional funds exclude tobacco companies
  regardless of valuation.
- FMCG-others segment still operates at thinner margins than the legacy
  cigarette business, so the earnings mix matters for valuation multiples.
