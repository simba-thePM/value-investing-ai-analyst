## Business Overview
Titan, a Tata Group company, is India's largest branded jewellery retailer
(Tanishq, Mia, CaratLane) and also has a large watches and wearables
business (Titan, Fastrack) plus a smaller eyewear segment (Titan Eye+).

## Competitive Moat
- **Trust in an unorganized-dominated category**: Jewellery in India is
  still largely sold through small, local, unbranded jewellers; Tanishq's
  guaranteed purity (hallmarking) and no-questions-asked exchange policy
  give it a strong trust advantage that is hard for local players to match
  at scale.
- **Brand portfolio segmentation**: Different brands (Tanishq for premium,
  Mia for young working women, CaratLane for digitally-native buyers)
  capture different customer segments without cannibalizing each other.
- **Distribution scale**: The largest organized jewellery retail network in
  India, with continued store expansion into smaller towns.
- **Working capital discipline**: Sophisticated gold sourcing and
  hedging practices relative to smaller regional competitors.

## Capital Allocation
High historical return on capital employed (ROCE) in the jewellery
business, funded largely through internal accruals and disciplined store
expansion; a track record of successfully incubating new brands
(CaratLane, Mia) rather than only relying on the core Tanishq brand.

## Management Quality
Tata Group governance standards, long-tenured category leadership (notably
in building Tanishq into a category leader over 25+ years), consistent
capital discipline messaging to investors.

## Risks
- High revenue and margin sensitivity to gold price volatility and import
  duty changes.
- Category is discretionary consumer spending, sensitive to economic
  slowdowns.
- Increasing competition from other large groups (e.g. Reliance Retail,
  Malabar Gold) entering organized jewellery retail aggressively.
